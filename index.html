PhD student. Working in various digital humanities projects at the Centre for Information Modelling at the University of Graz. I studied history and digital heritage studies (EuroMACHS).
My PhD is about ontology-based resource discovery. I am very interested in semantic technologies, linked open data and web development in the humanities.
I grew up in a village in Upper Styria in Austria.

Bio & Membership & Events Organised

Bio

Holds a Master degree in History and a Joint Master’s Degree in Digital Heritage (EuroMACHS). Currently he is a PhD candidate in Digital Humanities. Since 2016 he has been a research assistant at the Centre for Information Modelling (ZIM) at the University of Graz. His work includes technical development and data modelling in various projects like STEFAN ZWEIG DIGITAL, ‘Open Access Database Adjective-Adverb Interfaces in Romance’ or ‘Digital Edition Publishing Cooperative for Historical Accounts’. The main focus of his work is on semantic web technologies, resource discovery, and web programming. In addition to teaching in these fields, his dissertation project focuses on information-based resource discovery for research data in the humanities.
